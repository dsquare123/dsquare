Republic Day

Republic day is celebrated to honor the day on which the constitution of India came into force on 26th January 1950, replacing the Government of India Act (1935) as the Governing document of India. Every year this auspicious occasion is celebrated in front of the Administrative block of the National institute of technology, Calicut. The head of the institution hoists the National Flag accompanied by the National Anthem and addresses the gathering by remembering and paying respect to our brave leaders and freedom fighters who struggled a lot to the 'Purna Swaraj' in India. NCC cadets of NITC do the Republic day parade. The head of the institution moves to the NCC group for taking the Guard of Honour with the NCC Cadet Captain.

The NCC Cadets Salute the National Flag followed by the March past. The vote of thanks is given by the Registrar of the institute.

Annual Training Camp

Each year Annual Training Camp is held for NCC cadets. These are held within the state under the aegis of respective NCC Directorates. Various kinds of activities are organised in this camp such as Physical training, Marching and drill, Firing, Map Reading, Weapon Training, Cultural activities, Ship visits, Lectures regarding organization of Indian Armed Forces, Armed Forces Ranks & Uniform, Health & Hygiene, etc., and Awareness Seminars regarding Cyber security & Drug abuse.

Weapon Training

NCC Cadets are taught Weapon Training during the Camps like ATC, CATC,etc. Weapon Training is a very important component of NCC programme. Training of shooting with Riffles is an integral part of this programme. The fine art of shooting, teaches a person Precision, accuracy, co-ordination of body movements, patience and confidence which all helped to make him a better man in the public life. The shooting at the Ranges helps an individual to master this as a sport and also to learn handling of a weapon for self-protection.

Weapon training includes Characteristics of .22 rifle, Stripping, Assembling, Care & Cleaning, sight setting, Loading, Cocking, Unloading ,Aiming I-Range & Targets, Range procedure and safety precautions.

Map Reading NCC cadets are taught Map Reading during Camps (like ATC CATC, etc.) by the PI Staffs.  

Map reading is the process of looking at the map to determine what is depicted and how the cartographer depicted it. This involves identifying the features or phenomena portrayed, the symbols and labels used, and information about the Map that may not be displayed on the Map, The Grid System, Relief Contours & Gradients, Cardinal Points & Finding North, Types of Bearing & Use of Service Protractor, Prismatic Compass, its Use & Introduction to GPS, Setting a Map, Finding Own Position & North, Map to Ground, Ground to Map, Point to Point March etc. are the contents included in Map Reading.

Rashtriya Ekta Divas

Rashtriya Ekta Divas is observed on 31st October in India and is also known as National Unity Day. It was introduced by the Government of India in 2014. The day honours the legacy of Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel. He is also known as the India's Iron Man. He was a key figure in the National Integration. The official statement for National Unity Day by the Home Ministry of India cites that the National Unity Day "will provide an opportunity to re-affirm the inherent strength and resilience of our nation to withstand the actual and potential threats to the unity, integrity and security of our Country". NCC NITC cadets honoured him with a general salute.

National Anthem was sung. Rashtriya Ekta Divas was celebrated inside the Administrative Block of NITC. The head of the institute addressed the gathering.

Mega Swatchata Pakhwada

To Commemorate the 150th Birth Anniversary of Mahatma Gandhi the NCC Directorate Kerala & Lakshadweep conducted a Mega Swachata Pakhwada on 28th September 2019.
As we know Environmental conservation is the burning issue of modern times and it is the duty of individuals to realise the obligation towards the society. The “Mega Swachhta Pakhwada” is an event to get the attention of the public to make them aware about the importance of trees. A clean environment is essential for a better tomorrow. The main purpose of this Programme was to create awareness among our students about the need and importance of clean environment.

The NCC cadets of 9 (k) Naval Unit NITC participated and cleaned the area near the Academic Block and Main Canteen in the NITC Institute.
